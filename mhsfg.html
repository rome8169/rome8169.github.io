Mixed Hodge Structures on Fundamental Groups

Michaelmas, 2019

Schedule: 2pm to 4pm, Mondays; C3, Mathematical Institute

Course Description: This is a graduate course. The main purpose is to study periods of fundamental groups. For this purpose, one needs a de Rham theory for fundamental groups. Such a de Rham theory has been developed by K. T. Chen using iterated integrals. By defining weight and Hodge filtrations on iterated integrals, Hain puts a natural mixed Hodge structure on the fundamental group. This Hodge theory is particularly useful if one wants to study how periods vary in families (in particular asymptotic/limit behavior towards infinity), or study periods (of fundamental groups) of moduli spaces of curves, e.g. polylogarithms and their elliptic analogues.
We will select from the topics covered in Hain's website. Our plan is to first define iterated integrals, discuss Chen's \(\pi_1\)-de Rham theorem, and basics of mixed Hodge theory. Then we combine these to put mixed Hodge structures on fundamental groups. We will then spend some time talking about limit mixed Hodge structures. Afterwards, we will focus on applications and be example-oriented. Depending on interests and time constraint, we can do either or both of the following: (i) compute regularized periods; (ii) understand the mixed Hodge structure on \(H^1(SL_2(\mathbb Z), Sym^n H)\).

Prerequisites: We assume basics of algebraic topology: fundamental groups and (co)homology. Facts of spectral sequences and homotopy theory will be used occasionally, but prior knowledge is not necessary.
